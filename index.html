Not your usual finance guy.

I am Jonty. My degree is in sport and exercise science, first class, which is not the standard route into personal finance. But I go deep on whatever I do not yet understand, and I have a low tolerance for problems that should not exist. Money kept supplying both.

It started when I had to track my own finances across two currencies inside a British tax system, and nothing out there did it properly. So I taught myself to build and shipped Vault, a privacy first finance app that does. Building it forced me to understand ISAs, capital gains, pensions and crypto tax well enough to explain them to a computer. Explaining them to a camera turned out to be the easy part.

Since then I have backpacked Thailand, Laos and Vietnam, and I am writing this from Melbourne. Earning in one currency, living in another, funded by the boring money habits I talk about online. That is the whole pitch. I am not rich and I am not selling a course. I am building optionality in public and showing my working.

Three numbers your bank hopes you never compare.

The number they tell you
3%

Inflation

The official rise in the price of your shopping. Annoying, visible, endlessly reported.

The number they don't
8%

Debasement

Money printing quietly shrinking every pound you hold. Nobody puts this one on the news.

The number that matters
11%

The hurdle rate

Roughly inflation plus debasement. What your money must earn each year just to stand still.

Cash sitting in a savings account is not being safe. It is quietly evaporating. Once you see the hurdle, everything else follows.

Illustrative figures, not financial advice. I explain the workings in the content.
